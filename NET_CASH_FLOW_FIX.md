# Annual Contribution → Income Column Fix

## The Problem

After age 65, the table showed positive "Annual Contribution" (like $100k) even though you're no longer earning a salary. This was confusing because it implied you were still working and saving money.

**Why it happened:**
- Your **Social Security ($60k/year)** + **Pension ($40k/year at age 65)** = **$100k income**
- The calculation labeled this as "contribution" since `income - spending` was still positive
- This made it unclear that you're on retirement income, not salary

## The Fix

The table now shows **your total passive income** in that column after retirement:

| Column | Before Age 52 | After Age 65 |
|--------|---------------|--------------|
| "Income" | Your salary ($270k) | Social Security + Pension ($100k) |

### What This Means for You

**Before Retirement (Age 32-51):**
- Shows your salary income minus spending = what you're saving each year
- Positive number = building retirement nest egg

**After Retirement (Age 65+):**
- Now shows your **passive income** (SS + Pension)
- This is the amount your portfolio can safely support via withdrawal rate
- You're no longer "contributing" - you're receiving income

## Updated Table Layout

```
| Age | Portfolio | Net Cash Flow | Annual Spending | Income Sources | Phase  |
|-----|-----------|----------------|-----------------|----------------|--------|
| 60  | $X        | ($50k)         | $200k           | Salary only    | Working|
| 65  | $Y        | $0             | $180k           | SS + Pension   | Retired|
```

**The "Net Cash Flow" column now shows:**
- **Positive**: You're saving (salary > spending)
- **Zero**: Income exactly covers spending
- **Shows your income source**: After retirement, you'll see SS + Pension amounts

## Key Takeaway

After turning 65, the column will show approximately **$100k** - this is your annual passive income from Social Security ($60k) and pension ($40k). Your portfolio no longer needs to generate income for basic living - it's your "safety net" withdrawal source if spending exceeds this passive income.
