# Life Events & Net Cash Flow Fix

## The Issue You Identified

The "Net Cash Flow" column was showing **income** (Social Security + Pension) but **NOT accounting for life events spending** like daycare and college expenses. This was misleading - especially after age 65 when you might have significant ongoing life event costs.

## What Was Fixed

### The Column Now Shows:
```
Net Cash Flow = Income - ALL Spending
               = (Social Security + Pension) - (Base Spending + Life Events + Health Care + Debt Payments)
```

### Color Coding Added:
- **Green (#2e7d32)**: Positive net cash flow → income exceeds spending
- **Red (#c62828)**: Negative net cash flow → spending exceeds income

## Example Scenarios

### Age 65 (No Life Events):
```
Income: SS ($60k) + Pension ($40k) = $100k
Spending: Base ($168k)
Net Cash Flow: -$68k ❌ (red - spending exceeds income)
```

### Age 35 (With Daycare & College):
```
Income: Salary ($270k)
Spending: Base ($144k) + Daycare ($24k) + College ($4.8k) = $172.8k
Net Cash Flow: -$2.8k ❌ (red - but smaller negative due to higher salary)
```

## Why This Matters

**Before the fix:**
- Column showed income ($100k after retirement) without context of total spending needs
- Could mislead you into thinking your portfolio was being funded positively when life events were burning through it

**After the fix:**
- Shows TRUE net cash flow considering ALL expenses including life events
- Color coding makes it instantly clear if income is sufficient
- Helps you identify when you need to: reduce spending, increase retirement contributions, or adjust life event timelines

## Example Fix in Action

With daycare ($24k/year) and college savings (~$50k total over years):
```
Age 65 with Daycare Costs:
Income: $100k (SS + Pension)
Spending: $170k + Life Events ($24k) = $194k
Net Cash Flow: -$94k ❌ (red - you're spending far more than passive income!)

This reveals that daycare costs alone burn through nearly 10% of your passive retirement income!
```

## Key Takeaway

The column now accurately reflects whether your income can sustain all your spending including life events. After turning 65, if the column shows negative amounts, you're relying on your portfolio growth to make up the difference - which is why FIRE planning focuses on building enough assets to generate sustainable withdrawals.
